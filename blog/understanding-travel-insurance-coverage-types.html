Understanding Travel Insurance Coverage Types

Informational Guide IRDAI Compliant 5 min read

Individual Travel Insurance

Individual policies cover a single traveller and are the most common type of travel insurance. Coverage includes medical emergencies, trip cancellation, baggage loss, flight delays, and personal accidents. Sum insured typically ranges from USD 50,000 to USD 1,000,000 depending on destination and policy. Premiums are calculated based on age, destination, trip duration, and sum insured selected.

Family Travel Insurance

Family floater policies cover you, your spouse, and dependent children under a single plan. These are often more economical than buying individual policies for each family member. Children up to a certain age are covered under the parents’ policy at no additional cost, making family plans ideal for holiday travel.

Student Travel Insurance

Designed specifically for Indian students going abroad for higher education. Key features include higher medical coverage limits, tuition fee loss protection, emergency travel for parents, and study interruption coverage. Many foreign universities require proof of student insurance before enrolment.

Senior Citizen Travel Insurance

Travellers aged 60 and above have specific needs. Senior plans offer higher medical cover limits, coverage for acute onset of pre-existing conditions, and enhanced medical evacuation benefits. Premiums are higher due to increased health risks at older ages.

Domestic Travel Insurance

Covers travel within India including trip cancellation, baggage loss, and travel delays. Useful for travellers booking non-refundable tickets or carrying valuable luggage. Premiums are generally affordable.

Annual Multi-Trip Insurance

For frequent travellers, a single annual policy covers all trips within 12 months, subject to a maximum duration per trip. This option often costs less than purchasing multiple single-trip policies over the course of a year.

💡
Note: Coverage details vary between insurers. Travellers should review individual policy documents carefully before purchasing.
