Medical Coverage in Travel Insurance: What's Included?

Informational Guide IRDAI Compliant 5 min read

Medical Emergency Coverage

Medical coverage is the most important component of any travel insurance policy. It covers inpatient hospitalisation, outpatient doctor visits, diagnostic tests, ambulance charges, prescription medicines, and nursing services required due to sudden illness or accident during your trip. Sum insured typically ranges from USD 50,000 to USD 1,000,000 depending on the policy and destination.

Medical Evacuation and Repatriation

If adequate medical facilities are unavailable at your travel location, the insurer arranges and pays for evacuation to the nearest suitable medical facility. In the unfortunate event of death during travel, repatriation of mortal remains to India is covered, including all necessary transportation costs.

Dental Emergencies

Most travel insurance policies cover emergency dental treatment for sudden and severe tooth pain, dental infections, or accidental damage to natural teeth. Coverage is usually limited and does not cover routine check-ups or pre-existing dental conditions.

Prescription Medicines

Coverage for prescribed medications during an emergency medical event. Keep original prescriptions and pharmacy receipts for claim submission.

24/7 Medical Assistance Services

All travel insurance policies include round-the-clock emergency assistance services including help finding medical providers, translator services, coordination with hospitals, and emergency cash advances when needed most.

💡
Important: Pre-existing medical conditions are generally excluded. Some policies offer limited coverage for acute onset of pre-existing conditions for travellers aged 60 and above. Review policy wording carefully.
