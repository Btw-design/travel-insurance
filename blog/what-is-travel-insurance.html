What is Travel Insurance? A Complete Guide for Indian Travellers

Informational Guide IRDAI Compliant 6 min read

What is Travel Insurance?

Travel insurance is a specialised insurance policy designed to cover financial losses and medical emergencies that may occur while travelling. For Indian travellers going abroad, it serves as a safety net against unexpected events such as medical emergencies, trip cancellations, lost baggage, flight delays, and personal accidents.

Travel insurance policies in India are regulated by the Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India (IRDAI), ensuring standardised practices across all licensed insurers. Policies are available for both domestic and international travel, with international travel insurance being more comprehensive due to higher medical costs abroad.

Why Do Indian Travellers Need Travel Insurance?

  • Medical Emergencies Abroad: Healthcare costs in countries like the USA, UK, Singapore, and UAE are significantly higher than in India. A single hospital visit can cost lakhs of rupees without insurance.
  • Mandatory for Visa Applications: Many countries require proof of travel insurance as a condition for issuing a visa, particularly Schengen countries, the USA, Australia, and several Southeast Asian nations.
  • Trip Cancellation or Interruption: If you need to cancel or cut short your trip due to unforeseen circumstances, travel insurance can reimburse non-refundable expenses such as flight tickets and hotel bookings.
  • Lost or Delayed Baggage: Airlines sometimes lose or delay baggage. Travel insurance provides compensation for essential purchases and lost belongings.
  • Flight Delays: Many policies cover expenses incurred during prolonged flight delays, including meals, accommodation, and alternate transport arrangements.
  • Personal Liability: If you accidentally cause injury to someone or damage property abroad, personal liability coverage can protect you from legal and financial consequences.

Types of Travel Insurance Available in India

  • Single-Trip Policy: Covers one specific trip from departure to return, ideal for occasional travellers.
  • Annual Multi-Trip Policy: Covers multiple trips within a year, typically with a maximum duration per trip of 30 to 90 days.
  • Student Travel Insurance: Designed for Indian students studying abroad, offering long-term coverage with tuition fee protection.
  • Senior Citizen Travel Insurance: Tailored for travellers aged 60 and above, with enhanced medical coverage and pre-existing condition options.
  • Domestic Travel Insurance: Covers travel within India including trip cancellation, baggage loss, and travel delays.
💡
Important: Travel insurance is a regulated product in India. All policies must comply with IRDAI guidelines regarding disclosure, claim settlement timelines, and policyholder rights.
