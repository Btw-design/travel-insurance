How to File a Travel Insurance Claim: Step-by-Step Guide

Informational Guide IRDAI Compliant 6 min read

Understanding the Claim Process

Filing a travel insurance claim can seem daunting, but understanding the procedure in advance helps ensure a smooth experience. The claim process varies depending on the type of claim — medical emergency, baggage loss, trip cancellation, or flight delay. Under IRDAI guidelines, insurers must have a transparent and efficient claim settlement process.

Step 1: Notify the Insurer Immediately

As soon as an event occurs that may lead to a claim, notify your insurer or their 24/7 assistance provider. Most policies require notification within 24 to 48 hours of the incident. For medical emergencies, the assistance team can guide you to network hospitals and coordinate direct billing.

Step 2: Gather Required Documents

  • Medical Claims: Original medical bills, discharge summary, prescriptions, doctor’s certificate, police report if applicable, passport copies with immigration stamps.
  • Baggage Loss or Delay: Property irregularity report from the airline, boarding passes, baggage tags, receipts for essential purchases.
  • Trip Cancellation: Original booking documents, cancellation invoices, supporting evidence such as medical certificates or visa refusal letters.
  • Flight Delay: Boarding passes, flight delay certificate from the airline, expense receipts.

Step 3: Submit the Claim

Claims can be submitted online through the insurer’s portal, via email, or by post. Ensure all documents are scanned clearly. Keep copies and note the claim reference number for tracking.

Step 4: Claim Processing and Settlement

Under IRDAI regulations, claims must be settled within 30 days of receipt of all required documents. If investigation is required, settlement must happen within 45 days. If rejected, the insurer must provide a written explanation citing the specific exclusion.

Tips for a Smooth Claim

  • Read your policy wording thoroughly before travelling.
  • Keep insurance certificate and emergency numbers accessible during your trip.
  • For theft or loss, file a police report immediately.
  • Submit all documents as soon as possible after returning to India.
💡
IRDAI Guidelines: If unsatisfied with the claim decision, approach the insurer’s Grievance Redressal Officer, then IRDAI IGMS at igms.irdai.gov.in , then the Insurance Ombudsman at ecoi.co.in .
