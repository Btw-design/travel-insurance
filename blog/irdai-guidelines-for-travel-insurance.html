IRDAI Guidelines for Travel Insurance: What You Must Know

Informational Guide IRDAI Compliant 5 min read

Role of IRDAI in Travel Insurance

The Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India (IRDAI) is the supreme regulatory body governing the insurance sector. All travel insurance products sold in India must comply with IRDAI regulations designed to protect policyholder interests and ensure fair treatment.

Key IRDAI Regulations

  • Standardisation of Products: Insurers must file product wordings with IRDAI for approval before launch to ensure minimum coverage levels.
  • Claim Settlement Timelines: Claims must be settled within 30 days (45 days for investigation). Rejected claims require a written explanation.
  • Free Look Period: Policyholders have 15 days from receipt to review the policy and return it for a refund if unsatisfied.
  • Disclosure Requirements: All terms, conditions, exclusions must be clearly disclosed in simple language.
  • Portability: Policyholders can switch insurers while maintaining continuity of benefits.
  • Grievance Redressal: Every insurer must have a grievance mechanism. Unresolved issues can be escalated to IGMS and the Insurance Ombudsman.

Grievance Redressal Process

  • Level 1: Contact the insurer’s Grievance Redressal Officer.
  • Level 2: File a complaint on IRDAI IGMS at igms.irdai.gov.in .
  • Level 3: Approach the Insurance Ombudsman at ecoi.co.in .
  • Level 4: Reach out to Bima Bharosa at bimabharosa.irdai.gov.in .
💡
Remember: IRDAI regulations are designed to protect your interests. Familiarising yourself with these rules empowers you to make informed decisions.
