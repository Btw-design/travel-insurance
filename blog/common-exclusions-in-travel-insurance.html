Common Exclusions in Travel Insurance Policies

Informational Guide IRDAI Compliant 5 min read

Understanding Exclusions

Every travel insurance policy includes a list of exclusions — specific events, circumstances, or conditions that are not covered. Understanding these exclusions is essential to avoid unpleasant surprises during the claim process and to plan accordingly for additional coverage where needed.

Common Standard Exclusions

  • Pre-Existing Medical Conditions: Any medical condition that existed before the policy start date is generally excluded. Some senior policies offer limited acute onset coverage.
  • Adventure Sports: Bungee jumping, scuba diving, skydiving, mountaineering, paragliding, and similar high-risk activities are excluded unless you purchase an add-on adventure sports cover.
  • Self-Inflicted Injuries: Injuries resulting from suicide attempts, self-harm, or reckless behaviour are excluded.
  • Alcohol and Drug Use: Claims arising from intoxication or substance abuse are not covered.
  • War and Terrorism: Losses caused by war, civil unrest, or terrorism may be excluded or limited.
  • High-Risk Destinations: Travel to countries with active government advisories or sanctions may be excluded.
  • Unattended Baggage: Loss or damage to baggage left unattended in public places is not covered.
  • Electronics and Valuables: Many policies impose sub-limits on expensive items like laptops, cameras, and jewellery.
  • Normal Pregnancy: Routine pregnancy and childbirth costs are excluded. Some policies cover emergency complications.
  • Nuclear Events: Damage from nuclear radiation or contamination is excluded.

How to Avoid Exclusion Issues

  • Read the policy wording carefully before purchasing.
  • Disclose all relevant information honestly during the application.
  • Consider add-on covers for adventure sports or valuable items.
  • Contact the insurer for clarification if unsure about specific exclusions.
💡
Important: This is a general list. Exclusions vary by insurer and policy. Always refer to the specific policy document for complete terms and conditions.
