Basics

General Questions About Travel Insurance

Travel insurance is a specialised insurance policy that covers financial losses and medical emergencies that may occur while travelling. It works by providing reimbursement or direct payment for covered expenses such as medical treatment, trip cancellation, lost baggage, flight delays, and personal accidents. When you purchase a policy, you pay a premium based on factors like your age, destination, trip duration, and the sum insured. If a covered event occurs during your trip, you file a claim with supporting documents, and the insurer reimburses you or pays the provider directly.

Travel insurance is mandatory for visa applications to Schengen area countries, the USA, Australia, Canada, the UAE, and several other nations. These countries require proof of travel insurance with minimum medical coverage as a condition for visa approval. Even when travel insurance is not mandatory for a particular destination, it is strongly recommended for financial protection against medical emergencies and travel disruptions abroad. Medical costs in many countries can be extremely high, and without insurance, a single hospital visit could cost lakhs of rupees.

You should purchase travel insurance as soon as you book your trip, ideally at least 7 to 15 days before your departure date. Buying early ensures that you are covered for pre-departure cancellations due to unforeseen events such as illness, visa refusal, or family emergencies. Some travellers make the mistake of waiting until the last minute, which means they lose coverage for any events that occur between booking and purchasing the policy. Early purchase also gives you adequate time to review the policy terms and understand the coverage.

The cost of travel insurance varies depending on several factors including your destination, age, trip duration, and the sum insured you select. As a general guideline, a typical 15-day trip to Europe for a 30-year-old traveller may cost between INR 500 and INR 2,000 for a standard policy. Senior citizens, travellers visiting countries with high medical costs like the USA, longer trip durations, and higher coverage limits will all result in higher premiums. It is advisable to compare policies from multiple IRDAI-registered insurers to find coverage that suits your needs and budget.

Most travel insurance policies must be purchased before you depart from India. Some insurers may offer limited coverage options for travellers who are already abroad, but these typically have reduced benefits, higher premiums, and more exclusions. It is strongly recommended to purchase your policy before departure to ensure comprehensive coverage from the start of your journey. If your trip is extended while you are abroad, many insurers allow you to extend your existing policy by paying an additional premium, provided you request the extension before the original policy expires.

Coverage

Coverage & Benefits Questions

Standard travel insurance policies typically cover medical emergencies and hospitalisation expenses, trip cancellation and interruption costs, loss or delay of checked baggage, flight delays, personal accidents resulting in death or disability, personal liability for injury or damage caused to others, and emergency medical evacuation to the nearest appropriate medical facility. Some comprehensive policies also include coverage for passport loss, hijacking, and additional expenses incurred due to travel disruptions. It is important to note that specific coverage details vary by insurer and policy, so you should always review the policy document carefully.

Generally, pre-existing medical conditions are excluded from standard travel insurance policies. A pre-existing condition is any medical condition, illness, or injury that existed or for which you received treatment or medication before the policy start date. However, some policies, particularly senior citizen travel insurance plans, may offer limited coverage for the acute onset of pre-existing conditions. Acute onset means a sudden and unexpected worsening of a stable condition that requires immediate medical attention. It is crucial to disclose all your medical conditions honestly when purchasing a policy, as non-disclosure can lead to claim rejection.

Standard travel insurance policies generally exclude adventure sports and high-risk activities such as bungee jumping, scuba diving beyond a certain depth, skydiving, paragliding, hang gliding, mountaineering above a specified altitude, white water rafting, and similar activities. If you plan to engage in any of these activities during your trip, you should consider purchasing an add-on adventure sports coverage rider, which is offered by many insurers at an additional premium. This rider extends coverage to include injuries and accidents resulting from participation in specified adventure sports.

Medical cover limits in travel insurance policies typically range from USD 50,000 to USD 1,000,000 depending on the policy, destination, and premium paid. For travellers visiting countries with expensive healthcare systems like the USA, Canada, or Singapore, higher coverage limits of USD 500,000 or more are recommended. Many insurers offer multiple coverage tiers, allowing you to choose a sum insured that matches your destination and risk profile. Higher coverage limits naturally come with higher premiums, but the additional cost is usually modest compared to the financial protection provided.

Many insurers allow you to extend your travel insurance policy if your trip is extended unexpectedly, provided you request the extension before the original policy expiration date. An additional premium is charged based on the number of extra days required. It is important to contact your insurer’s assistance team as soon as you know your trip will be extended to ensure continuous coverage without any gaps. If you wait until after the policy expires, you may not be able to extend and would need to purchase a new policy, which may not cover pre-existing conditions that arose during the original trip.

Claims

Claim Process & Settlement Questions

To file a travel insurance claim, follow these general steps: First, notify your insurer or their 24/7 assistance provider immediately after the incident occurs. Second, gather all required documents relevant to your claim type — medical bills for health claims, police reports for theft, airline reports for baggage issues, and cancellation invoices for trip cancellation claims. Third, submit the completed claim form along with supporting documents through the insurer’s online portal, email, or postal address. Fourth, note the claim reference number provided by the insurer for future tracking and follow up if you do not receive a response within the expected timeframe.

Under IRDAI regulations, insurance companies must settle valid claims within 30 days of receiving all required documents from the policyholder. If the insurer needs to conduct an investigation into the claim due to its complexity or suspected fraud, the settlement must be completed within 45 days. If the insurer fails to settle the claim within these prescribed timelines, they are liable to pay interest on the claim amount at the rate of the bank rate plus 2 percent per annum for the period of delay. These timelines are designed to ensure that policyholders receive their claim amounts promptly.

If your travel insurance claim is rejected, the insurer must provide you with a written explanation citing the specific policy exclusion or reason for rejection. If you believe the rejection is unfair or incorrect, you have the right to escalate the matter through the following IRDAI-mandated grievance redressal process: First, approach the insurer’s designated Grievance Redressal Officer with a formal complaint. Second, if unsatisfied with the response, file a complaint on the IRDAI Integrated Grievance Management System (IGMS) at igms.irdai.gov.in . Third, approach the Insurance Ombudsman at ecoi.co.in for independent dispute resolution. Fourth, reach out to Bima Bharosa at bimabharosa.irdai.gov.in for further assistance.

For a medical travel insurance claim, you typically need the following documents: original medical bills and hospital invoices, discharge summary from the hospital, doctor’s prescriptions and medical certificates, diagnostic test reports, police report if the injury resulted from an accident or third-party incident, copies of your passport pages with immigration stamps showing entry and exit dates, a copy of your insurance certificate or policy document, and the completed claim form provided by the insurer. Having all these documents ready and organised will help expedite the claim processing time.

The IRDAI grievance redressal process consists of four levels: Level 1 involves contacting the insurer’s internal Grievance Redressal Officer, who must acknowledge your complaint and provide a resolution within a specified timeframe. Level 2 involves filing a complaint on the IRDAI Integrated Grievance Management System (IGMS) at igms.irdai.gov.in if you are unsatisfied with the insurer’s response. Level 3 involves approaching the Insurance Ombudsman at ecoi.co.in for independent adjudication of your complaint. Level 4 involves reaching out to Bima Bharosa at bimabharosa.irdai.gov.in for further escalation. Each level should be approached sequentially after the previous one fails to resolve the issue.

Students & Seniors

Special Traveller Questions

Yes, student travel insurance is significantly different from regular travel insurance. Student policies are designed for Indian students going abroad for higher education and offer long-term coverage typically ranging from one to four years. They include higher medical coverage limits, tuition fee protection if studies are interrupted due to medical emergencies, coverage for emergency travel by a parent if the student is hospitalised, study interruption benefits, and personal liability coverage. Many foreign universities require students to have valid insurance before they can enrol, and student travel insurance is specifically designed to meet these requirements.

Yes, several Indian insurance companies offer travel insurance policies for senior citizens up to 75, 80, or even 90 years of age, depending on the insurer. Premiums for senior travellers are higher due to increased health risks, and some policies may require medical underwriting or a health declaration before issuing the policy. Senior-specific plans typically offer higher medical cover limits, coverage for acute onset of pre-existing conditions, and enhanced medical evacuation benefits. It is advisable to compare policies from different insurers and read the terms carefully, paying special attention to age limits, coverage for pre-existing conditions, and claim settlement reputation.

Domestic travel insurance is not mandatory for travel within India, but it can be useful in certain situations. It covers trip cancellation or interruption due to unforeseen events, loss or delay of checked baggage, and travel delays. If you frequently book non-refundable flight or train tickets, or if you travel with expensive luggage, electronics, or business equipment, domestic travel insurance provides financial protection against losses. Some comprehensive domestic policies also offer limited medical coverage, though many travellers rely on their regular health insurance for medical needs within India. Premiums for domestic travel insurance are generally quite affordable.

Student travel insurance policies offer several additional benefits beyond standard travel insurance. These include tuition fee protection, which reimburses fees for the affected semester if the student must return to India due to a medical emergency or death of an immediate family member. They also provide coverage for emergency travel expenses for a parent to visit if the student is hospitalised for an extended period. Other benefits include study interruption coverage for additional costs if studies are disrupted, mental health support and counselling services, coverage for laptops and academic materials, and higher personal liability limits suitable for student life abroad.

Yes, annual multi-trip travel insurance policies typically have age limits, with most insurers offering them to travellers up to 65 or 70 years of age. Some insurers offer annual policies for senior citizens up to 75 or 80 years, but these may require medical screening and come with higher premiums. Annual multi-trip policies also impose a maximum duration per trip, usually ranging from 30 to 90 consecutive days. If you are a frequent traveller over the age of 65, it is advisable to compare annual policies from multiple insurers, as coverage offerings and age limits can vary significantly between companies.
